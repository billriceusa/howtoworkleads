## 1. Newsletter Brand Guide

### Voice & Tone

**Who we sound like:** A top-producing agent who figured out aged leads five years ago, now sharing the playbook with a friend over coffee. Direct, practical, zero fluff.

- **Authoritative but not academic.** We have the data. We skip the jargon.
- **Empathetic to the grind.** We know what it feels like to buy 200 leads and close 3. We talk about what actually works, not what sounds good in a webinar.
- **Specific over vague.** Numbers, scripts, screenshots, conversion rates. Every issue gives the reader something they can use today.
- **Conversational cadence.** Short paragraphs. Rhetorical questions. Occasional one-sentence paragraphs for emphasis.

**Words we use:** conversion rate, contact rate, ROI, follow-up, close, pipeline, dial, connect, appointment, aged lead, data, scrub, DNC, cadence, drip, speed-to-lead.

**Words we avoid:** synergy, leverage (as a verb), cutting-edge, revolutionary, game-changer, unlock (unless referring to an actual lock).

### Format & Structure

Every issue follows this template:

```
FROM: Bill Rice <bill@howtoworkleads.com>
SUBJECT: [Punchy, specific subject line — see calendar]

---

## The Aged Lead Playbook — Issue #XX

**One-liner teaser** (1 sentence that sells the open)

---

### THIS WEEK'S PLAY (Main Content — 400-600 words)

The core tactical content. One focused topic. Includes:
- A specific tactic, script, data point, or framework
- Real numbers when possible (conversion rates, cost-per-lead, ROI)
- Step-by-step when applicable

### THE SCRIPT / THE TEMPLATE / THE DATA (Actionable Insert)

A copy-paste script, a checklist, a comparison table, or a mini case study.
Rotates based on content type. Always something the reader can screenshot or print.

### FROM THE BLOG (Site Link)

2-3 sentence teaser + link to a relevant howtoworkleads.com article.
Drives repeat traffic and engagement signals.

### THE STACK (Quick Hits — 3-4 bullets)

- Industry stat or trend
- Tool recommendation
- Compliance update
- Community question or poll result

---

**CTA:** [Rotates — see calendar. Always one clear CTA per issue.]

**Footer:**
The Aged Lead Playbook by HowToWorkLeads.com
Unsubscribe | Forward to a colleague who buys leads
```

### Design Notes

- Plain-text-forward design. Minimal images. Fast load on mobile.
- Brand yellow (#FFD500) used sparingly — CTA buttons only.
- No hero image. Subject line and first line do the selling.
- Mobile-first: 90%+ of agents read email on their phone between calls.

## 4. Growth Tactics

### On-Site Tactics

**Inline signup forms (every article)**
- Place a contextual signup box after the first H2 in every blog post and landing page.
- Copy matches the page topic: insurance pages promote Insurance Scripts Bundle, mortgage pages promote Mortgage Scripts Bundle, general pages promote Quick-Start Kit.

**Exit-intent popup**
- Trigger: mouse leaves viewport (desktop) or 60-second timer (mobile).
- Offer: "Get the Aged Lead Quick-Start Kit — free."
- Suppress for 30 days after close. Suppress permanently after signup.

**Sticky bottom bar**
- Persistent bar on all pages: "Join 2,000+ agents getting the Aged Lead Playbook every Tuesday."
- Collapse on scroll-down, expand on scroll-up.

**Content upgrades**
- Each major article has a content-specific upgrade (the scripts bundle, cadence template, or scorecard).
- Inline CTA: "Want the copy-paste version? Download the [specific asset]."

**ROI Calculator email gate**
- Calculator is free to use. Full report with benchmarks requires email.
- "Enter your email to get your personalized ROI report + weekly tips."

**Blog post footer**
- Every blog post ends with: "This is the kind of tactic we share every Tuesday in The Aged Lead Playbook. [Subscribe free]."

### Off-Site Tactics

**LinkedIn distribution**
- Repurpose each newsletter issue as a LinkedIn post (Tuesday afternoon, after email send).
- Format: key insight from the issue + "Full breakdown in this week's Aged Lead Playbook (link in comments)."
- Target insurance agent and mortgage LO LinkedIn groups.

**Facebook group seeding**
- Identify 10-15 active Facebook groups for insurance agents and mortgage LOs.
- Share genuine value (answer questions, share data). Link to articles, not directly to signup.
- Groups to target: Insurance Agents & Brokers, Final Expense Agents, Mortgage Loan Officers, IMO/FMO groups.

**Reddit participation**
- r/InsuranceAgent, r/LifeInsurance, r/MortgageLoanOfficer, r/sales
- Answer questions about lead buying. Link to relevant guides when organic.

**Podcast guest strategy**
- Pitch insurance and mortgage industry podcasts.
- Topic: "Why the smartest agents are buying aged leads in 2026."
- Drive listeners to a custom landing page with lead magnet.

**Cross-promotion swaps**
- Partner with complementary newsletters (CRM tools, insurance marketing, mortgage coaching).
- Swap dedicated sends or "recommended by" mentions.

**Quora and forum answers**
- Answer questions about aged leads, lead buying, insurance/mortgage lead generation.
- Link to relevant howtoworkleads.com articles as source.

### Engagement Optimization

**Subject line testing**
- A/B test every subject line (50/50 split, winner after 2 hours, send to remainder).
- Test formats: number vs. no number, question vs. statement, short vs. long.

**Send time optimization**
- Start at Tuesday 8 AM ET. Test 7 AM, 9 AM, and 10 AM in months 2-3.
- Insurance agents dial early. Mortgage LOs check email mid-morning. Find the sweet spot.

**Reply triggers**
- End 1 in 4 issues with a question that invites replies: "What's your contact rate on aged leads? Hit reply — I read every one."
- Replies boost sender reputation and deliverability.

**Segmentation (Month 3+)**
- Tag subscribers by lead magnet downloaded: insurance, mortgage, general.
- Occasionally send vertical-specific bonus content to each segment.

**Re-engagement sequence**
- 60 days no open: send "Still working leads?" re-engagement email.
- 90 days no open: send "Should I remove you?" last-chance email.
- 100 days no open: move to inactive. Stop sending.

**Welcome sequence (5 emails over 10 days)**
1. Day 0: Deliver lead magnet + "Here's what to expect every Tuesday"
2. Day 2: Best-of article link ("Start here: Aged vs Fresh Leads")
3. Day 4: Second lead magnet offer (different from signup asset)
4. Day 7: ROI calculator invite + "What vertical are you in?" (segmentation)
5. Day 10: Social proof + "Forward this to a colleague who buys leads"
