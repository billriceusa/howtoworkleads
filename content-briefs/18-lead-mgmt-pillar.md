## Outline

### H1: Lead Management: The Complete Guide to Organizing, Tracking & Converting Prospects

### H2: What Is Lead Management?
- Definition: the process of capturing, qualifying, nurturing, and converting leads into customers
- Why it matters: without a system, 80%+ of leads go unconverted
- The difference between lead management and a CRM (link to child page)
- Who needs lead management: sales teams, insurance agents, mortgage LOs, service businesses

### H2: The Lead Management Process (5 Steps)
1. **Lead Capture** — how leads enter your system (web forms, purchased lists, referrals)
2. **Lead Qualification** — determining fit and readiness (BANT, budget, timeline)
3. **Lead Distribution** — routing leads to the right rep (round-robin, geography, specialty)
4. **Lead Nurturing** — multi-touch follow-up until they're ready to buy
5. **Lead Conversion** — closing the deal and tracking attribution

### H2: The Lead Lifecycle — 7 Stages
- Brief overview with link to dedicated child page
- New → Contacted → Qualified → Nurturing → Opportunity → Customer → Advocate
- Why understanding the lifecycle prevents premature follow-up abandonment

### H2: Lead Management for Internet Leads vs. Referrals
- Internet leads: higher volume, lower trust, require more touches
- Referrals: warmer but less predictable, still need systematic follow-up
- The hybrid approach: using a lead management system for both

### H2: Lead Management for Aged Leads Specifically
- Why aged leads require a DIFFERENT management approach
- Pipeline separation: never mix aged and fresh leads in the same pipeline
- Cadence differences: aged leads need patience, fresh leads need speed
- Link to aged leads hub and cadence post

### H2: Choosing a Lead Management System
- What to look for: multi-channel sequences, pipeline management, reporting, automation
- CRM vs. standalone lead management tools
- Link to CRM comparison post and GHL setup guide

### H2: Lead Management Best Practices
- Never delete a lead — move to long-term nurture instead
- Track everything: calls, emails, texts, responses, outcomes
- Set SLAs: respond within X hours, follow cadence within X days
- Regular pipeline hygiene: review stale leads weekly
- Measure what matters: contact rate, response rate, conversion rate, revenue per lead

### H2: Common Lead Management Mistakes
- No system at all (relying on memory or sticky notes)
- One-and-done follow-up (calling once and giving up)
- Treating all leads the same (fresh vs. aged, internet vs. referral)
- Not tracking data (can't improve what you don't measure)
- Switching lead sources too quickly without sufficient data
