## Competitive Analysis

- Major HELOC lenders (Figure, Spring EQ, Bethpage) focus on product marketing, not LO conversion tactics.
- No guide addresses HELOC lead conversion from a pipeline and sales process perspective.
- HELOC prospects are fundamentally different — they already own a home, have equity, and are comparing HELOC vs. cash-out refi vs. home equity loan.
- The variable rate objection is the #1 barrier in the current rate environment.
- Urgency creation is key — HELOC prospects procrastinate because there's no transaction deadline.

- Explaining variable rate mechanics (index + margin, caps, floors)
- Draw period vs. repayment period lifecycle
- Interest-only vs. P&I during draw period
- Rate comparison: HELOC variable vs. cash-out fixed vs. HEL fixed

### H2: Urgency Triggers for HELOC Prospects
- Why HELOC leads procrastinate (no transaction deadline, no seller waiting)
- Creating legitimate urgency: rate environment changes, equity fluctuation, project timelines
- The "cost of waiting" calculation
- Seasonal urgency (home improvement season, tax planning, back-to-school)

### H2: Follow-Up Cadence for HELOC Leads
- HELOC prospects are slower to act than purchase borrowers
- Education-heavy cadence with equity market updates
- Re-engagement hooks: rate changes, new products, equity increases
- Extended nurture (90-day minimum)

### H2: Common Objections and Handling
- "I'm worried about the variable rate"
- "I'd rather do a cash-out refinance"
- "I'm not sure I need the money right now"
- "My current rate is too low to touch"
- "I'll just use a credit card or personal loan"

### H2: Pipeline Management for HELOC Leads
- Pipeline stages (equity estimation through funded line)
- HELOC-specific milestones (appraisal, title, draw activation)
- Tracking draw utilization post-close for referral timing
- Cross-sell opportunities (rate-and-term refi, investment property)

### H2: Conversion Benchmarks and Revenue Math
- Contact rate, qualification rate, close rate for HELOC leads
- Revenue per close: origination fees + ongoing interest income (portfolio lenders) or flat fee
- HELOC as door-opener to full mortgage relationship
- ROI model for aged HELOC leads

### H2: FAQ
1. **What's the best opening line for a HELOC lead?** [Lead with equity, not rate: "Based on your home value, you may be sitting on $X in accessible equity"]
2. **How do I overcome the variable rate objection?** [Show the rate cap structure, compare total cost to alternatives, and explain the draw period flexibility advantage]
3. **Should I push HELOC or cash-out refi?** [Depends on their existing first mortgage rate — if it's below current market, HELOC preserves that rate]
4. **What's the typical close timeline for a HELOC?** [14-30 days for most lenders, some fintech lenders close in 5-7 days]
5. **How do aged HELOC leads perform compared to fresh?** [Strong — homeowners with equity don't lose that equity over time, and the need for funds often increases]
