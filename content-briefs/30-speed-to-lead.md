## Outline

### H1: Speed to Lead: Why Contact Timing Matters — Even for Aged Leads

### H2: The "Speed to Lead" Myth (and the Truth)
- The famous stat: respond within 5 minutes and you're 21x more likely to qualify a lead
- Why this stat is about FRESH leads only — and misleads aged lead buyers
- Aged leads don't care if you call 5 minutes or 5 hours after import — they filled out the form weeks or months ago
- The REAL "speed to lead" for aged leads: how quickly you execute your CADENCE after importing

### H2: What "Speed" Actually Means for Aged Leads
- Speed of system setup: have your cadence, scripts, and CRM ready BEFORE buying leads
- Speed of initial contact: start the cadence within 24 hours of importing leads
- Speed of follow-through: complete the full 7-day cadence without gaps
- Speed of response: when a lead REPLIES (email, text, call back), respond within minutes — THIS is where speed matters

### H2: The Best Time of Day to Call Aged Leads
- Mid-morning: 10:00-11:30 AM (lead's local time) — highest answer rate
- Early evening: 4:30-6:30 PM — second-best window (people off work)
- Avoid: before 9 AM, during lunch (12-1), after 8 PM
- Why: aged leads are going about their day — you're interrupting, not responding
- Vary your call times across the cadence (don't always call at 10 AM)

### H2: The Best Day of Week to Call Aged Leads
- Tuesday-Thursday: highest contact rates
- Monday: people are catching up, lower answer rates
- Friday: checked out for the weekend, moderate
- Saturday: surprisingly good for some industries (insurance, B2C)
- Sunday: generally avoid (except strategic voicemail drops)

### H2: The Best Time to Send Aged Lead Emails
- Tuesday-Thursday: highest open rates
- 9-10 AM local time: email checked at start of workday/morning routine
- Avoid: Monday AM (inbox overload), Friday PM (weekend mode)
- Test and iterate: your audience may differ from averages

### H2: The Best Time to Send Aged Lead Texts
- Mid-day: 11 AM-1 PM — casual, less intrusive
- Evening: 5-7 PM — after work, checking phone
- Never: before 8 AM or after 9 PM (TCPA compliance + basic courtesy)
- Keep texts brief and send during waking hours (lead's time zone!)

### H2: Response Speed — The One Place Speed DOES Matter
- When an aged lead replies to your email → respond within 15 minutes
- When an aged lead calls back → answer live or return within 10 minutes
- When an aged lead texts back → respond within 5 minutes
- Why: their interest is a spark — respond fast or it dies
- This is the REAL "speed to lead" for aged leads: speed of RESPONSE, not speed of first contact

### H2: Timing Strategies by Industry
- **Insurance:** Call during weekday daytime, email Tuesday-Thursday AM
- **Mortgage:** Call early evening (LOs are working during the day, borrowers available evening)
- **Solar:** Saturday mid-morning is excellent (homeowners are home, thinking about projects)
- **Final expense:** Weekday mid-morning (elderly demographic, early risers, home during the day)

### H2: Setting Up Time-Optimized Automation
- CRM workflow triggers based on time zones
- Scheduled sends: queue emails/texts for optimal delivery windows
- Call task scheduling: create tasks for ideal call windows
- Link to GHL setup guide for technical implementation

### H2: FAQ
1. **Does "speed to lead" apply to aged leads?** [Not in the traditional sense — but speed of cadence execution and response to callbacks is critical]
2. **What's the single best time to call aged leads?** [10-11 AM local time, Tuesday-Thursday]
3. **How quickly should I respond to an aged lead callback?** [Within 10-15 minutes — treat it like a fresh lead at that moment]
4. **Should I call aged leads on weekends?** [Depends on industry — Saturday works for B2C, avoid Sunday]
5. **Can I automate contact timing in my CRM?** [Yes — most CRMs support time-zone-aware scheduling]
