# How to Work Purchase Mortgage Leads: The Loan Officer's Playbook

Purchase mortgage leads are the most consistent revenue source in the lending business — but most loan officers work them wrong. They call once, leave a voicemail, and move on. Or they launch into a rate pitch before understanding where the borrower is in their homebuying journey. Or they treat a purchase lead like a refinance lead and miss the urgency signals entirely.

Purchase leads are fundamentally different from every other mortgage lead type. They're driven by life events, not interest rates. They involve a parallel process — the borrower is also finding a home, working with a real estate agent, and navigating inspections and appraisals. And they have a built-in conversion tool that no other lead type offers: pre-approval.

This guide gives you the complete system for working purchase mortgage leads — qualification framework, scripts, 7-day cadence, objection handling, and the pre-approval strategy that separates top-producing LOs from everyone else.

## Why Purchase Leads Need Their Own Playbook

If you've been working [aged mortgage leads](/blog/how-to-work-aged-mortgage-leads) with a one-size-fits-all approach, you're leaving money on the table. Purchase leads require a fundamentally different strategy because of four dynamics:

**Life-event motivation.** Refinance borrowers are motivated by numbers — a lower rate, a better payment. Purchase borrowers are motivated by life changes — a new job, a growing family, a marriage, a desire to stop renting. These emotional drivers create urgency that numbers alone don't produce.

**The agent variable.** Most purchase borrowers are simultaneously working with (or looking for) a real estate agent. The LO who connects with the borrower first often gets introduced to the agent — and the LO who has an agent relationship can capture borrowers in both directions. This dual-channel dynamic doesn't exist in refinance or HELOC lending.

**Pre-approval as a conversion weapon.** Pre-approval is the single most valuable thing you can offer a purchase borrower. It tells them how much they can afford, it makes them competitive in a seller's market, and it locks them into working with you through the transaction. No equivalent tool exists for refinance or HELOC leads.

**Timeline urgency.** Purchase timelines are often driven by external factors — a lease ending, a school year starting, a job relocation date. These hard deadlines create natural urgency that keeps the borrower engaged. Refinance borrowers can postpone indefinitely.

## The Purchase Lead Qualification Framework

The first 60 seconds of any purchase lead conversation should qualify the borrower across five dimensions. Get these answers and you'll know exactly how to prioritize and work the lead.

### The Five-Question Framework

**1. Timeline: "When are you looking to buy?"**

This is the most important question. The answer segments your leads into three buckets:

- **Active (0-3 months):** High priority. These borrowers are actively looking at homes. They need pre-approval now. Put them at the top of your pipeline.
- **Planning (3-6 months):** Medium priority. They're getting ready but not actively shopping. Get them pre-approved early and set up a monthly check-in cadence.
- **Exploring (6+ months):** Low priority for immediate follow-up, but add to your long-term nurture sequence. Monthly market updates and rate snapshots keep you top of mind.

**2. Pre-approval: "Have you been pre-approved by another lender?"**

If yes, you're competing against an established relationship. Your approach shifts to differentiation — better rates, better service, faster closing, or a specialty program they may not have been offered.

If no, you have the opportunity to be their first and only pre-approval. This is the ideal scenario.

**3. Agent: "Are you working with a real estate agent?"**

If yes, the agent is influencing the borrower's lender choice. Ask the agent's name and reach out to introduce yourself — many agents prefer LOs who proactively communicate.

If no, you have an opportunity to provide additional value: "I work with several excellent agents in [area]. Want me to connect you with someone after we get your pre-approval done?"

**4. Down payment: "How much have you saved for a down payment?"**

This tells you the borrower's financial readiness and which programs to recommend (conventional, FHA, VA, USDA, down payment assistance). Borrowers with 3-5% down need different guidance than those with 20%.

**5. Credit: "Any concerns about qualifying that I should know about?"**

This open-ended question invites the borrower to share issues — collections, late payments, recent credit events — without feeling interrogated. It also signals that you're a problem-solver, not just a rate quoter.

### The 60-Second Qualification Script

Here's how it flows in practice:

"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company]. I'm a loan officer in [area] and I'm following up on your mortgage inquiry. First question — are you still thinking about buying a home? ... Great. What's your timeline looking like — are you actively shopping, or more in the planning phase? ... And have you been pre-approved by a lender yet? ... Perfect. I'd love to get you a free pre-approval so you know exactly what you qualify for. It takes about 15 minutes, and there's no obligation. What's a good time to walk through that?"

## The 7-Day Follow-Up Cadence for Purchase Leads

Adapt the standard [aged lead follow-up cadence](/blog/aged-lead-follow-up-cadence) for purchase mortgage leads with these daily touchpoints:

### Day 1: Call + Text

**Call (morning, 10-11 AM local time):**

"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company]. I'm following up on your home purchase inquiry. I wanted to see if you're still looking, and if so, offer you a free pre-approval so you know exactly what you qualify for. Give me a call back at [number] — it takes about 15 minutes and puts you in a much stronger position when you find the right home."

**Text (if no answer, sent 2 hours after call):**

"Hi [Name] — this is [Your Name], loan officer at [Company]. I tried calling about your home purchase inquiry. I can get you pre-approved in about 15 minutes with no obligation. Want to set up a quick call? [Your Number]"

### Day 2: Email

**Subject:** Your free mortgage pre-approval is ready

"Hi [Name],

I reached out yesterday about your home purchase inquiry. I wanted to share a quick snapshot of where rates are right now:

- 30-year fixed: [current rate]%
- FHA 30-year: [current rate]%
- VA 30-year: [current rate]% (if applicable)

More importantly, I can get you pre-approved in about 15 minutes — no obligation, no cost. A pre-approval letter shows sellers you're a serious buyer and gives you clarity on your budget before you start shopping.

Would you like to set up a quick call? Just reply to this email or call me at [number].

[Signature]"

### Day 3: Call (Different Time) + Text

**Call (evening, 5-6 PM local time):**

Try a different time of day. If you called in the morning on Day 1, try the evening. Many borrowers are more available and relaxed after work.

"Hi [Name], I tried you a couple of days ago about your home purchase. I'm still happy to get you that free pre-approval whenever you're ready. Rates are looking [favorable/stable] right now. Call me at [number] when you have 15 minutes."

**Text (if no answer):**

"Hi [Name] — [Your Name] here. Still happy to help with your home purchase. Pre-approval takes 15 min and it's free. Let me know when works. [Number]"

### Day 5: Email with Value Content

**Subject:** Home buying checklist (so you don't miss anything)

Send an educational email with genuine value — a homebuying checklist, a mortgage payment calculator link, or a local market snapshot. The goal is to demonstrate expertise and stay top of mind without being pushy.

"Hi [Name],

Whether you end up working with me or another lender, I wanted to share a quick homebuyer checklist that covers the key milestones from pre-approval to closing. [Link or inline checklist]

If you'd like to discuss your specific situation, I'm here. Just reply or call [number].

[Signature]"

### Day 7: Call + Breakup Voicemail

**Call (morning):**

"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] — I've reached out a few times about your home purchase inquiry. I don't want to be a bother, so this will be my last call for now. If you're still thinking about buying a home, my offer for a free pre-approval stands — just give me a call at [number] whenever you're ready. I hope to hear from you."

The breakup voicemail works because it creates subtle urgency (last chance) while being respectful. It often triggers callbacks from prospects who meant to respond earlier.

### Extended Nurture (Beyond Day 7)

For purchase leads who don't convert during the initial cadence, move them to a monthly drip:

- **Monthly rate snapshot** — Current rates with a brief "what this means for your buying power" note
- **Local market update** — New listings, price trends, inventory levels in their target area
- **Seasonal tips** — "Best time to buy in [area]," "How to compete in a spring market," etc.
- **Re-engagement trigger** — If rates drop significantly, run a campaign: "Rates just dropped — your buying power just increased by $X"

## Scripts for Purchase Mortgage Leads

### Cold Call Opener (Aged Lead)

"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company]. I'm following up on a mortgage inquiry you submitted a while back about purchasing a home. I know some time has passed, but I wanted to check in — are you still thinking about buying? ... Great. A lot has changed in the market since then, and I'd love to get you updated on what you qualify for. It takes about 15 minutes and there's no cost or obligation."

### Voicemail Script

"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] at [Company], [phone number]. I'm a mortgage specialist in [area] and I'm reaching out about your home purchase inquiry. I can get you pre-approved in about 15 minutes — no cost, no obligation, and it gives you a real number to work with when you're house hunting. Give me a call back at [number]. That's [number]. Talk soon."

### Text Templates

**First contact:** "Hi [Name] — [Your Name], loan officer at [Company]. Following up on your home purchase inquiry. I can get you pre-approved in ~15 min with no obligation. Interested? [Number]"

**Follow-up:** "Hi [Name] — just checking back. Still happy to help with your home purchase. Rates are at [X]% right now. Pre-approval is free and takes 15 min. [Number]"

**Rate trigger:** "Hi [Name] — rates just dropped to [X]% which means more home for your budget. Want to get pre-approved and see what you qualify for? [Number]"

### Objection Handling

**"I already have a lender."**

"That's great — it's smart to be working with someone. Would it be helpful to get a second opinion on your rate and terms? Sometimes a quick comparison can save you thousands over the life of the loan. It takes 15 minutes and there's no obligation."

**"I'm just looking / not ready yet."**

"No problem at all. When do you think you might be ready to start actively looking? ... Perfect. Would it be helpful if I got you pre-approved now so you know your budget, and then checked in with you around [their timeline]? Pre-approval letters are usually valid for 60-90 days."

**"Rates are too high."**

"I hear that a lot. Here's the thing — buying when rates are higher often means less competition and more negotiating power on price. And you can always refinance to a lower rate later. Would you like to see what your payment would look like at today's rates? You might be surprised."

**"I need to talk to my spouse / think about it."**

"Absolutely — this is a big decision. I'll send you a quick summary of what we discussed so you can share it with [spouse]. What's the best email for that? And would it be okay if I checked back in on [specific day]?"

## The Pre-Approval Advantage

Pre-approval is your single most powerful conversion tool for purchase leads. Here's how to maximize it:

### Why Pre-Approval Matters to Borrowers

- **Clarity:** They know exactly how much they can afford before shopping
- **Competitiveness:** A pre-approval letter makes their offer stronger in a competitive market
- **Confidence:** The uncertainty of "will I qualify?" is removed
- **Speed:** When they find the right home, they can move immediately

### Positioning Pre-Approval as Value, Not Commitment

Some borrowers hesitate because they think pre-approval means they're locked in. Address this:

"Pre-approval doesn't commit you to anything — it's simply a letter that confirms what you qualify for. You're free to shop around, take your time, or not buy at all. But having that letter in your pocket means when you find the right home, you can move fast."

### Soft Pull vs. Hard Pull

This is a common concern. Be transparent:

"I can do a preliminary assessment with a soft credit pull that doesn't affect your score. If everything looks good and you want to move forward with a formal pre-approval, we'll do a hard pull at that point. The hard pull impact is minimal — typically 5-10 points — and it's temporary."

### Using Pre-Approval to Lock the Relationship

Once a borrower is pre-approved with you, they're invested. They have your letter, they know their number, and switching to another lender means starting the process over. This is your structural advantage — get the pre-approval done and you'll retain the vast majority of these borrowers through closing.

## Working Aged Purchase Leads vs. Fresh

### Aged Purchase Leads

The key difference with aged purchase leads: re-qualify the timeline. A borrower who indicated they wanted to buy "in the next 3 months" six months ago is either past their original timeline or their plans shifted. Your opening should acknowledge the time gap:

"I know you submitted this inquiry a while back. I wanted to check — are you still thinking about buying, or has your situation changed?"

Possible responses and what they mean:

- **"We already bought."** Dead lead. Congratulate them and ask for referrals.
- **"We're still looking."** Active lead — proceed with qualification and pre-approval.
- **"We put it on hold."** Nurture lead — ask what changed and when they might revisit, then add to monthly drip.
- **"Who is this?"** Expected. Re-introduce yourself, explain why you're calling, and offer value (pre-approval).

### Fresh Purchase Leads

Speed matters — call within 5 minutes of receiving the lead. The borrower just filled out a form and is in active research mode. Be the first voice they hear. Your conversion rate drops dramatically with every minute of delay.

With fresh leads, skip the "checking in" positioning and go straight to value: "I see you're looking to buy a home in [area]. Let's get you pre-approved right now so you have a real number to work with. It takes about 15 minutes."

## Common Mistakes LOs Make with Purchase Leads

**Quitting too early.** The average LO makes 1.5 contact attempts before giving up. Top producers make 7-10 attempts across multiple channels. Your competition is quitting — persistence is your edge.

**Leading with rate instead of pre-approval.** Rate is important, but it's not what a purchase borrower needs to hear first. They need to know what they qualify for. Lead with pre-approval, then discuss rate.

**Ignoring the agent relationship.** When a borrower has a real estate agent, that agent is influencing the lender decision. Proactively reach out to the agent, introduce yourself, and make it easy for them to work with you.

**Not adjusting for aged leads.** Calling an aged lead with the same urgency as a fresh lead sounds tone-deaf. Acknowledge the time gap, re-qualify the timeline, and offer value.

**Failing to nurture long-term.** Many purchase borrowers take 3-12 months to buy. If your follow-up ends at Day 7, you're missing the majority of closeable leads. Set up automated monthly nurture and stay top of mind.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the best opening line for a purchase mortgage lead?

Lead with a pre-approval offer: "I'd love to get you pre-approved so you know exactly what you qualify for — it takes about 15 minutes and there's no cost or obligation." Pre-approval is the most valuable thing you can offer a homebuyer, and it immediately positions you as a resource rather than a salesperson.

### How do I compete with online lenders for purchase leads?

Local expertise, speed, and the agent relationship. Online lenders can compete on rate but rarely on responsiveness, local market knowledge, or agent partnership. Highlight that you're a local professional who can close on time, communicate proactively, and support the borrower through a complex process — things online lenders struggle to deliver consistently.

### Should I work purchase leads from states I'm not licensed in?

Do not originate loans in states where you lack an active MLO license — this is a federal and state regulatory violation. Instead, refer the lead to a licensed loan officer in that state and negotiate a referral fee (typically 25-50 basis points). This turns unlicensable leads into passive income rather than wasted investment.

### How many purchase leads should I work at once?

A single loan officer can effectively manage 200-500 aged leads per month when using automation for email and text sequences and focusing live calls on the highest-priority leads (active timeline, warm responses). If you're also working fresh leads, reduce aged lead volume to maintain response speed on fresh leads.

### What's the biggest mistake LOs make with purchase leads?

Not following up enough. Data consistently shows that most leads are contacted 1-2 times before being abandoned. Conversion happens between attempt 5 and attempt 10 for most lead types. Build a minimum 7-touch cadence across call, email, and text — and add monthly nurture for leads that don't convert in the first week.
