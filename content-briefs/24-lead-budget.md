## Outline

### H1: Insurance Agent Lead Budget: How Much Should You Spend on Leads in 2026?

### H2: Why Most Agents Get Lead Budgeting Wrong
- Spending too little (not enough volume for a valid test)
- Spending too much on the wrong type (all fresh, no system)
- No tracking (spending blind without ROI measurement)
- The "invest in a system first, then scale spend" approach

### H2: The Lead Budget Formula
- **Target monthly income** ÷ **average commission per sale** = **sales needed**
- **Sales needed** ÷ **close rate** = **leads needed**
- **Leads needed** × **cost per lead** = **monthly lead budget**
- Walk through 3 examples:
  - Solo agent, $10K/month target, life insurance
  - Solo agent, $10K/month target, final expense
  - Small team, $50K/month target, multi-line

### H2: Budget by Business Stage

#### H3: New Agent (0-6 Months)
- Start with aged leads only: $200-$500/month
- Goal: learn the system, build skills, track metrics
- 500-2,000 aged leads per month
- Expected ROI: breakeven or slight profit — investing in learning

#### H3: Established Solo Agent (6-24 Months)
- Mix aged + fresh: $500-$2,000/month
- Split: 70% aged / 30% fresh
- Goal: consistent pipeline, predictable income
- Expected ROI: 200-400%

#### H3: Growing Agency (2+ Years / Team)
- Scale budget to rep count: $1,000-$3,000 per rep per month
- Separate budgets for aged vs. fresh
- Add live transfers and exclusive leads selectively
- Expected ROI: 300-500% with mature systems

### H2: Budget by Lead Type

| Lead Type | Monthly Budget (Solo) | Leads/Month | Expected Sales |
|-----------|---------------------|-------------|---------------|
| Aged (30-90 day) | $200-$600 | 200-600 | 4-18 |
| Aged (90-365 day) | $100-$300 | 200-1,000 | 2-10 |
| Fresh / Real-Time | $500-$2,000 | 20-100 | 3-15 |
| Live Transfers | $1,000-$3,000 | 10-30 | 5-15 |

### H2: The Test Budget — Start Here
- Minimum viable test: 500 aged leads ($500-$1,500)
- Work them for 30 days using a real cadence
- Track: contact rate, appointment rate, close rate, revenue
- Only after the test: decide whether to scale or adjust

### H2: Tracking Your Lead Spend ROI
- Simple spreadsheet: monthly spend, leads purchased, contacts made, appointments, closes, revenue
- The metrics that matter: CPA, revenue per lead, ROI percentage
- When to increase spend (ROI positive and capacity available)
- When to decrease spend (ROI negative or capacity maxed)

### H2: Common Budgeting Mistakes
- Buying 50 leads and calling it a "test" (statistically insignificant)
- Spending on leads before having a CRM and follow-up system
- Comparing aged lead performance to fresh lead expectations
- Cutting budget after one bad month (need 90-day window for evaluation)
- Not accounting for seasonal variation (enrollment periods, rate changes)

### H2: FAQ
1. **How much should a new insurance agent spend on leads?** [$200-$500/month on aged leads to start]
2. **What's the minimum number of leads for a fair test?** [500 aged leads, worked over 30 days]
3. **Should I buy aged or fresh leads on a tight budget?** [Aged — 10-50x more leads for the same money]
4. **How do I know if my lead spend is working?** [Track CPA and ROI — if CPA is below your average commission, you're profitable]
5. **How long should I wait before judging ROI?** [90 days minimum — insurance has longer sales cycles]
