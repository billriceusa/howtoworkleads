## Outline

### H1: Buy Annuity Leads: Quality Retirement Prospects for Financial Professionals

### H2: What Are Annuity Leads?
- Consumers who've expressed interest in annuity products for retirement income
- How they're generated: online financial planning calculators, retirement planning forms, comparison sites
- Typical prospect profile: 50-70 years old, $100K+ in assets, nearing or in retirement
- Higher-value sale than term life — commissions can be substantial

### H2: Types of Annuity Leads
- **Fixed annuity leads** — safe money, guaranteed rates, conservative prospects
- **Fixed indexed annuity (FIA) leads** — market participation with downside protection
- **Variable annuity leads** — investment-oriented, higher risk tolerance
- **MYGA leads** (Multi-Year Guaranteed Annuity) — CD alternative seekers
- **Income annuity leads** — pension-replacement seekers, lifetime income focused

### H2: Annuity Lead Pricing
- Fresh: $20-$50+ per lead (premium product = premium leads)
- Aged (30-90 days): $3-$8 per lead
- Aged (90-365 days): $1-$5 per lead
- Why annuity leads cost more than term life: higher ticket size, more qualified prospects

### H2: Aged vs. Fresh Annuity Leads
- Fresh: higher contact rate, prospects actively shopping
- Aged: dramatically cheaper, and annuity decisions are SLOW (people take months to decide)
- Key insight: annuity leads AGE WELL because the decision cycle is long — a 6-month-old annuity lead may still be undecided
- Link to aged vs. fresh comparison post

### H2: Where to Buy Annuity Leads
- AgedLeadStore (recommendation)
- What to look for: asset/income filters, age targeting (50+), geographic filtering
- Suitability: ensure lead data supports suitability documentation requirements

### H2: How to Work Annuity Leads
- Education-first approach (annuities are complex, prospects need guidance)
- Longer nurture required than insurance leads
- Seminars/webinars as conversion tool for aged annuity leads
- Key difference: you're selling a financial concept, not a simple product
- Link to insurance guide and scripts post for general framework

### H2: Compliance — Suitability Requirements
- FINRA suitability rules (if variable annuities)
- State insurance department requirements
- Documentation requirements
- Why compliance matters even more for annuity sales (larger ticket, more scrutiny)

### H2: FAQ
1. **How much do annuity leads cost?** [Fresh $20-$50+, aged $1-$8 depending on age]
2. **Are aged annuity leads worth it?** [Yes — long decision cycle means aged leads are often still in-market]
3. **What makes a good annuity prospect?** [50+, $100K+ assets, retirement-focused, conservative to moderate risk tolerance]
4. **How do I qualify an annuity lead quickly?** [Age, asset level, retirement timeline, risk tolerance — 4 questions]
5. **Do I need a securities license for annuity leads?** [Only for variable annuities (Series 6 or 7); fixed annuities require insurance license only]
