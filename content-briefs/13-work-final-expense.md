## Outline

### H1: How to Work Final Expense Leads: The Agent's Complete Guide

### H2: Why Final Expense Is a Unique Niche
- Demographics: 50-85 year olds, fixed income, phone-preferred
- Product simplicity: small policies ($5K-$25K), no medical exam (simplified/guaranteed issue)
- Emotional sale: protecting family from funeral burden
- High commission relative to premium

### H2: Understanding Your Final Expense Prospect
- Who they are: age, income level, family situation
- What motivates them: family protection, avoiding burden, peace of mind
- Communication preferences: phone over text/email, value patience and respect
- Common concerns: affordability, health qualifications, trustworthiness of agent

### H2: The Final Expense Follow-Up Cadence
- Adapted for older demographic (slower pace, more phone-heavy)
- Day 1: Call (AM preferred) + voicemail
- Day 2: Call (PM) + voicemail
- Day 3: Mail piece (if address available) — this demographic responds to physical mail
- Day 5: Call + text (keep text simple)
- Day 7: Call + simple email
- Day 10: Final call + handwritten note (if high-value prospect)

### H2: Final Expense Phone Scripts
- Opening: empathy-first, reference their inquiry, no pressure
- Qualifying: health questions (simplified issue), budget, family situation
- Presenting: keep it simple — monthly cost, coverage amount, what it covers
- Closing: "Would you like me to check what you qualify for right now?"
- The "I can't afford it" objection: break down to daily cost ($1-$2/day)

### H2: Voicemail and Email Templates
- Voicemail: warm, slow-paced, mention family protection
- Email: simple, large font recommended, clear CTA
- Note: many FE prospects don't use email — phone is primary

### H2: Telesales vs. Face-to-Face for Final Expense
- Telesales: higher volume, lower close rate, works for aged leads
- Face-to-face: higher close rate, limited geographic reach, better for fresh/exclusive leads
- Hybrid approach: qualify by phone, close in person for larger policies

### H2: Conversion Benchmarks
- Aged FE lead benchmarks: ~30% bad data, 3-4 appointments per 100, 1 sale per 100
- Revenue per lead calculations
- When to adjust approach vs. lead source
