### H1: The Sales Process for Internet Leads: Convert More Prospects Into Customers

### H2: What Is a Sales Process (And Why Most Lead Buyers Don't Have One)?
- Definition: a repeatable sequence of steps from first contact to closed deal
- Why internet leads demand a process: volume, speed, consistency
- The stat: sales teams with a defined process convert 33% more leads
- Link to "what is a sales process" child page

### H2: The B2C Internet Lead Sales Process (6 Steps)
1. **Lead Receives** — lead arrives, data captured, assigned to rep
2. **First Contact** — speed-to-lead (fresh) or multi-channel outreach (aged)
3. **Qualification** — determine fit: need, budget, timeline, authority
4. **Presentation** — deliver value: quote, proposal, demo, consultation
5. **Objection Handling** — address concerns systematically
6. **Close** — ask for the business, handle final objections, get commitment

### H2: How the Process Changes for Aged vs. Fresh Leads
- Fresh leads: Steps 1-2 must happen in MINUTES. Speed is everything.
- Aged leads: Steps 1-2 happen over DAYS. Persistence and value-first approach.
- Steps 3-6 are similar for both, but aged leads need more rapport-building
- Link to aged lead cadence and scripts posts

### H2: The Modern B2C Sales Funnel
- Brief overview of the funnel model (link to child page for depth)
- Top: awareness (they filled out a form)
- Middle: consideration (you're nurturing them)
- Bottom: decision (they're ready to buy)
- How internet leads enter at different funnel stages depending on age

### H2: Building a Repeatable Sales Process for Your Team
- Document every step (scripts, templates, timing, tools)
- Train reps on the process (not just the product)
- Measure at every stage (identify bottlenecks)
- Iterate: review monthly, adjust based on data
- The playbook concept: link to scripts and cadence posts

### H2: Sales Process vs. Sales Methodology
- Brief distinction with link to child page
- Process = steps (what you do)
- Methodology = philosophy (how you do it)
- Why you need both: the process is the skeleton, the methodology is the muscle

### H2: Common Sales Process Mistakes with Internet Leads
- No defined process at all
- One-size-fits-all approach (same process for aged and fresh)
- Skipping qualification (presenting before you know if they're a fit)
- Giving up too early (stopping after 1-2 contact attempts)
- Not tracking stage-to-stage conversion (can't find the bottleneck)
