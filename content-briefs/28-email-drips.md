## Outline

### H1: Aged Lead Email Drip Campaigns That Actually Work (With Templates)

### H2: Why Email Is Your Secret Weapon for Aged Leads
- Cheapest channel: pennies per email vs. minutes per call
- Most scalable: automate once, runs forever
- Non-intrusive: leads open on THEIR schedule
- The re-engagement power: email wakes up leads who would never answer a cold call
- The stat: email marketing generates $36 for every $1 spent (DMA)

### H2: The Aged Lead Email Strategy — It's NOT Just "Send More Emails"
- Aged leads require a different email approach than fresh leads
- Fresh lead emails: urgency, speed, "act now"
- Aged lead emails: value-first, re-education, trust-building, patience
- The goal of aged lead emails: re-engage, not close (close happens on the phone)

### H2: The 6-Email Aged Lead Drip Sequence

#### H3: Email 1 — The Reconnection (Day 1-2)
- Subject line examples: "Still thinking about [product]?" / "Quick question about your [insurance/mortgage] search"
- Body: Introduce yourself, reference their original inquiry, offer value (not a pitch), ask a simple question
- CTA: "Reply to this email" or "Book a quick call"
- Full template provided

#### H3: Email 2 — The Value Drop (Day 4-5)
- Subject line: "[Industry] tip that could save you $X" / "3 things most people don't know about [product]"
- Body: Share a genuinely useful tip, insight, or resource (educational, not sales-y)
- CTA: Link to a relevant blog post or resource
- Full template provided

#### H3: Email 3 — The Social Proof (Day 7-8)
- Subject line: "How [Name/role] saved $X with [product]" / "What other [agents/homeowners/etc.] are doing"
- Body: Short customer story or industry stat, positioning your solution as common and trusted
- CTA: "Want to see if this could work for you?"
- Full template provided

#### H3: Email 4 — The Personalized Check-In (Day 12-14)
- Subject line: "Checking in, [First Name]" / "Any questions I can answer?"
- Body: Short, personal, human — "I know life gets busy. Just wanted to check if [product] is still on your radar."
- CTA: "Just reply 'yes' or 'no' — I'll adjust accordingly"
- Full template provided

#### H3: Email 5 — The Update/News Angle (Day 18-21)
- Subject line: "[Industry] just changed — here's what you need to know" / "New [rates/options/savings] available"
- Body: Tie to a real market update (rate change, enrollment period, new product, regulatory news)
- CTA: "Want me to run new numbers for you?"
- Full template provided

#### H3: Email 6 — The Breakup Email (Day 28-30)
- Subject line: "Should I close your file?" / "Last email from me" / "Removing you from my list"
- Body: Polite but direct — "I don't want to be a pest. If the timing isn't right, no hard feelings. But if it is, this is the last time I'll reach out."
- CTA: "Reply if you'd like to stay in touch. Otherwise, I'll remove you from my contact list."
- Why this is the HIGHEST response rate email in the sequence
- Full template provided

### H2: After the 6-Email Sequence — Long-Term Nurture
- Move non-responders to monthly newsletter / market update drip
- Trigger-based re-engagement: if they open an email or click a link 3+ months later → restart active sequence
- Seasonal re-engagement: enrollment periods (insurance), rate drops (mortgage), spring (solar)

### H2: Email Drip by Industry

#### H3: Insurance Email Drip Variations
- Life insurance: protection angle, family security, policy comparison
- Health/ACA: enrollment period urgency, coverage comparison, cost savings
- Final expense: gentle, empathetic, family protection

#### H3: Mortgage Email Drip Variations
- Rate environment updates, pre-approval offers, market insights
- Purchase vs. refinance different angles

#### H3: Solar Email Drip Variations
- Utility bill savings, incentive deadlines, neighbor adoption stories

### H2: Subject Line Best Practices for Aged Leads
- Personalization (first name) increases open rates 26%
- Questions outperform statements
- Under 50 characters for mobile
- Avoid spam triggers: "FREE," "ACT NOW," all caps, excessive punctuation
- 5 subject line formulas that work

### H2: CAN-SPAM Compliance for Drip Campaigns
- Physical address in every email
- Unsubscribe link in every email
- Honor opt-outs within 10 days
- No misleading "From" name or subject line
