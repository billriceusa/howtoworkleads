## Outline

### H1: Aged Leads vs Fresh Leads: The Real Cost, ROI, and When to Use Each

### H2: What Are Fresh (Real-Time) Leads?
- Definition: leads delivered within seconds/minutes of consumer inquiry
- How they work: consumer fills out form → lead sold immediately
- Typical cost: $15-$50+ per lead depending on vertical
- Pros: high intent, high contact rate (60-80%)
- Cons: expensive, competitive (often sold to 3-5 buyers), narrow window

### H2: What Are Aged Leads?
- Definition: leads that are 30 days to 12+ months old
- Why they exist: unsold inventory, unconverted leads resold at discount
- Typical cost: $0.50-$5.00 per lead
- Pros: dramatically cheaper, buy in bulk, less competition
- Cons: lower contact rate (15-30%), requires different approach

### H2: ROI Analysis — When Aged Leads Win (and When They Don't)
- Break-even analysis: how many aged leads you need to close to match 1 fresh lead
- Example: If fresh lead costs $30 with 15% close rate = $200 CPA. Aged lead costs $2 with 2% close rate = $100 CPA. Aged wins 2:1 on CPA.
- When fresh leads make more sense: high-ticket products, time-sensitive offers, teams with limited follow-up capacity
- When aged leads make more sense: budget-conscious, strong follow-up system, volume-based business model

### H2: The Hybrid Approach — Why Smart Teams Use Both
- Use fresh leads for peak-intent moments
- Use aged leads to fill pipeline gaps and train new reps
- Budget allocation framework (e.g., 30% fresh / 70% aged for most insurance agents)
