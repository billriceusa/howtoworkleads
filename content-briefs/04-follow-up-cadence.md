## Outline

### H1: The 7-Day Aged Lead Follow-Up Cadence That Converts

### H2: Why Aged Leads Need a Different Follow-Up Rhythm
- Fresh lead cadence (speed-to-lead, 5 calls in 48 hours) doesn't work for aged
- Aged leads need: patience, multi-channel, value-first, lower pressure
- The stats: 80% of conversions happen between touch 5 and 12

### H2: The 7-Day Multi-Channel Cadence

**Visual table/diagram:**

| Day | Channel | Action | Goal |
|-----|---------|--------|------|
| 1 | Phone + Text | Call, leave voicemail if no answer, send intro text | First contact attempt |
| 2 | Email | Send value-based email #1 | Non-intrusive touchpoint |
| 3 | Phone | Call at different time of day, voicemail #2 | Second voice attempt |
| 4 | — | Rest day (let previous touches breathe) | — |
| 5 | Text + Email | Check-in text + email #2 with social proof | Multi-channel pressure |
| 6 | Phone | Call #3 + voicemail #3 | Third voice attempt |
| 7 | Email | Breakup email ("last time reaching out") | Trigger fear of missing out |

### H2: Days 8-14 — The Extended Follow-Up
- If no response in 7 days, shift to weekly cadence
- Day 10: One more call
- Day 14: Final breakup email
- After Day 14: Move to long-term nurture (monthly email drip)

### H2: Adjusting the Cadence by Lead Age
- **30-60 day leads:** Full intensity cadence (they may still be shopping)
- **60-180 day leads:** Standard cadence (they may have forgotten, need re-education)
- **180+ day leads:** Slower cadence (cast wider net, expect lower response rates)
- **Table showing adjusted timing for each age bracket**

### H2: Channel-Specific Best Practices
- **H3: Phone calls** — best times to call, voicemail best practices
- **H3: Text/SMS** — keep it short, identify yourself, compliance
- **H3: Email** — subject lines that work, avoid spam triggers, always include opt-out
- Link to scripts/templates post for actual script content

### H2: Setting This Up in Your CRM
- Brief overview of automation setup (don't go deep — link to CRM post)
- GoHighLevel: workflow builder setup
- Close CRM: sequence setup
- Generic: any CRM with multi-channel sequences

### H2: When to Stop — The Nurture Transition
- After the active cadence, don't delete the lead
- Move to long-term nurture: monthly email, quarterly check-in call
- Why: people buy when THEY'RE ready, not when YOU'RE ready
- Leads that seemed dead 6 months ago can convert

### H2: FAQ
1. **How many times should I call an aged lead?** [6-8 attempts across 2-3 weeks]
2. **What if they say they're not interested?** [Respect it — ask permission to follow up in 3 months]
3. **Should I use the same cadence for all lead types?** [Same structure, different scripts — adjust messaging by industry]
4. **Can I automate the entire cadence?** [Partially — automate email/text, keep calls manual]
5. **What's the average contact rate for aged leads?** [15-30% depending on lead age and data quality]
