## Key Legal Facts (Verified Against Authoritative Sources)

- FCC adopted the 1:1 consent rule in December 2023
- Scheduled effective date: January 27, 2025
- January 24, 2025: FCC issued 12-month stay; same month, 11th Circuit vacated the rule entirely
- Court ruling: FCC "impermissibly exceeded its statutory authority" — common law allows consent to multiple entities at once
- **The rule NEVER took effect**
- Multi-company consent with partner lists remains legally permissible
- Congress could pass similar legislation (courts only ruled FCC lacked authority, not that the rule was unconstitutional)
- Carrier SMS requirements (TCR) still effectively require 1:1 consent for text messaging
- Sources: Henson Legal, Mac Murray & Shuster LLP, DNC.com (Contact Center Compliance)
