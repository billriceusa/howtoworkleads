## Competitive Analysis

- Home improvement lead content is fragmented by trade (roofing, HVAC, windows, solar) with no unified lead-working framework.
- Contractor-focused content is heavily "marketing" oriented (how to generate leads) rather than "sales" oriented (how to convert leads you already have).
- Speed to lead is even more critical for contractors than insurance or mortgage — homeowners request multiple quotes simultaneously.
- No guide addresses aged lead re-engagement for contractors, which is a significant opportunity since home improvement needs don't expire — they escalate.
- Financing as a close tool is underutilized by most contractors and underdocumented in lead-working content.

### H2: FAQ
1. **How fast should I respond to home improvement leads?** [Within 5 minutes — homeowners request 3-5 quotes at once and book the first contractor who calls back]
2. **Do aged home improvement leads actually work?** [Yes — home improvement projects get delayed, not abandoned. A homeowner who needed a roof 6 months ago still needs a roof]
3. **Should contractors use a CRM?** [Absolutely — even a simple pipeline (new lead → appointment set → estimate given → follow-up → sold → scheduled) dramatically improves close rates]
4. **How do I reduce estimate no-shows?** [Confirmation text the morning of, reminder 1 hour before, and offer a specific arrival window rather than a vague time range]
5. **Does offering financing really help close more jobs?** [Yes — financing can increase close rates by 20-30%, especially for projects over $5,000 where the total cost creates sticker shock]
