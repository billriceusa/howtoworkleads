## Competitive Analysis

- Life insurance lead content is split between final expense (small policies, older prospects) and general life — no one bridges the full spectrum.
- The emotional selling angle is acknowledged but rarely operationalized with actual scripts and frameworks.
- Needs analysis is the key differentiator for life insurance agents, yet no competitor provides a step-by-step framework for phone-based needs analysis.
- Underwriting pre-qualification is a major conversion killer — agents who pre-qualify before submitting apps save time and improve placement rates.
- The term-to-permanent upsell and final expense cross-sell represent significant missed revenue for agents who only write one product type.

### H2: FAQ
1. **Should I lead with term or whole life?** [Lead with needs analysis — the product recommendation should follow from the prospect's situation, not your preference]
2. **How do I handle the "I need to talk to my spouse" objection?** [Schedule a three-way call — "Let's find a time when both of you can be on the line so I can answer all your questions together"]
3. **What's the biggest mistake agents make with life insurance leads?** [Pitching product before completing needs analysis — it signals you're selling, not advising]
4. **How do aged life insurance leads compare to fresh?** [Conversion is lower per lead but ROI is higher — the cost difference more than compensates]
5. **How do I cross-sell final expense to term life prospects?** [During needs analysis, address funeral costs separately — "Your term policy covers income replacement, but what about the immediate $10-15K in funeral and final expenses?"]
