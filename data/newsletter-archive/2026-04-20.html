THIS WEEK'S PLAY: The Medicare Pre-Season Strategy Nobody Talks About

Every Medicare agent knows AEP (October 15 - December 7). It's the Super Bowl. You prep your certifications, stock up on SOAs, and brace for the sprint.

But here's what the top producers do differently: they don't wait until October. They're building their pipeline in May.

The math behind off-season Medicare lead buying:

During AEP, fresh Medicare leads cost $20-$35 each. Vendors are slammed, lead quality is inconsistent, and every agent in your zip code is calling the same people.

Right now — in May — aged Medicare leads cost $0.40-$0.75 each. The people who requested information 60-120 days ago still have the same Medicare questions. They didn't buy a plan from the first agent who called (or that agent never followed up). And almost nobody is calling them.

You have 7 months until AEP. Here's the play:

Phase 1: May-July — Pipeline Building

Buy 200-500 aged Medicare leads at $0.40-$0.75 each. Run the 7-day cadence on each batch. Your goal isn't to sell a plan right now — it's to have conversations, build relationships, and identify who's:

  • Turning 65 this year (Initial Enrollment Period — you can sell now)
  • Unhappy with their current plan (AEP candidate)
  • On a Medicare Advantage plan that's changing networks
  • Approaching the coverage gap on Part D
Every one of these conversations goes into your CRM with notes and a follow-up date timed for September.

Phase 2: August-September — Warm-Up

Send a personal email or text to every contact from Phase 1: "Hey [Name], we spoke back in [month] about your Medicare coverage. Open enrollment starts October 15 — I wanted to check in and see if you'd like to review your options this year."

This message goes to people who already know your name and voice. Response rates on these warm contacts run 40-60% — compared to 15-25% on cold AEP leads.

Phase 3: October-December — Harvest

While other agents are cold-calling $30 leads, you're calling people who say "Oh hey, I remember you." Your AEP closes will cost a fraction of what the cold-call agents spend, and your conversion rate will be 2-3x higher.

What About T65 Leads?

Turning-65 leads are the one exception to the AEP timing play. Someone turning 65 has a 7-month Initial Enrollment Period — they need to act now, not in October. Aged T65 leads at 30-60 days old are your best immediate opportunity. These people are actively making a decision and many haven't finalized their plan yet.

### THE STACK

  • Stat: 11,000 Americans turn 65 every day. That's 4 million new Medicare-eligible individuals per year — and most of them requested information from at least one lead source before making a decision.
  • Compliance: CMS scope-of-appointment rules still apply to aged leads. You need a signed SOA before discussing specific plans. Build this into your cadence — Day 1 call books the appointment, confirmation email includes the SOA.
  • Calendar: Mark September 15 as your warm-up campaign launch date. Set a reminder now. The agents who win AEP decided to win it in May.
