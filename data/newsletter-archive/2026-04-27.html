THIS WEEK'S PLAY: Why Text-First Outreach Beats Cold Calling on Aged Leads

Here's a number that should change how you work aged leads: 98% of text messages are opened within 3 minutes.

Compare that to phone calls — where 75% go to voicemail — and email, where 20-30% open rates are considered good. Texts win, and it's not close.

But most agents still lead with a phone call. They dial, get voicemail, leave a message that gets deleted, and move on. Meanwhile, one text message could have started a conversation.

The text that gets callbacks:

> Hey [Name], this is [Your Name]. You requested some info about [insurance type / mortgage rates] a while back — just checking if you still need help with that. No pressure either way.

That's it. 32 words. Here's why it works:

1. It's personal. Their name + your name. Not a blast.
2. It references their action. "You requested info" — they did something, you're following up on it.
3. "A while back" — honest about the timing without being specific about how old the lead is.
4. "No pressure either way" — removes the sales tension. Counterintuitively, this increases response rates by 25-30% compared to texts without it.

5 Text Templates for Every Stage of the Cadence

Day 1 (after voicemail):
> Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] — I just left you a voicemail about your [product type] inquiry. Easier to text? Happy to help either way. Here's my number: [number]

Day 3 (standalone text):
> Hey [Name], following up on your [insurance/mortgage] request. Still looking? I can get you some numbers in about 5 minutes if so.

Day 5 (value-add):
> [Name] — quick heads up: [relevant fact, e.g., "rates dropped this week" or "Medicare open enrollment is coming up in October"]. If that affects your plans, happy to chat. — [Your Name]

Day 7 (breakup text):
> Hey [Name], I've reached out a few times about your [product] inquiry. Don't want to be a pest — if you're all set, totally understand. But if things change, you have my number. All the best. — [Your Name]

Day 14+ (long-tail nurture):
> Hi [Name], it's been a couple weeks since we connected about your [product type] request. If your situation has changed or you have questions, I'm still here. Just reply or call [number].

The Day 7 breakup text consistently has the highest callback rate — 15-20% on leads that were otherwise unresponsive. People respond to the door closing.

The Rules of Text Outreach

1. Always identify yourself. "This is [Name] from [Company]" in the first text. Anonymous texts get blocked.
2. One CTA per text. Don't ask three questions. Ask one.
3. Keep it under 160 characters when possible. Single-segment SMS messages have higher delivery rates than multi-segment ones.
4. Don't text before 8 AM or after 9 PM local time. TCPA rules and common sense.
5. Stop texting the moment they say stop. Immediately. No exceptions. This is legal, not optional.
6. Match their energy. If they respond with "yeah maybe," don't come back with a 200-word pitch. Short reply = short response.

### THE TEMPLATE

Text-first Day 1 approach (alternative to phone-first):

For agents who hate cold calling or work in verticals where phone answer rates are below 15%, try leading with text on Day 1 instead:

> Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company]. You inquired about [product] a little while back. If you still need help with that, I'd love to assist. Can I give you a quick call, or would you prefer to text?

Then call on Day 2 instead. By then, your name is already in their phone. Answer rates on Day 2 calls after a Day 1 text run 30-40% higher than cold calls.

### THE STACK

  • Stat: 89% of consumers say they prefer texting with businesses over calling. For adults under 45, that number is 96%. If your leads are under 55, text should be your primary channel.
  • Tool: GoHighLevel, Salesmsg, or Heymarket for compliant business texting with tracking. Never text leads from your personal phone — you need opt-out tracking and delivery receipts.
  • Compliance: The TCPA requires prior express consent to send marketing texts. Aged leads from reputable vendors include this consent — but keep the documentation. If someone replies STOP, honor it immediately and log it in your CRM.
