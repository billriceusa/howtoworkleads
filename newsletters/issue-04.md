## THIS WEEK'S PLAY: The CRM Setup That Stops Leads From Falling Through

Let me describe what I see in 90% of agents' CRMs:

One big bucket called "Leads." Maybe a second bucket called "Contacted." And a graveyard of records with no notes, no follow-up dates, and no idea where they came from.

That's not a CRM. That's a spreadsheet with a subscription fee.

If you're working aged leads at any volume (50+/month), your CRM isn't optional — it's the engine. A properly set up CRM does three things:

1. **Prevents leads from falling through the cracks** — automated follow-up sequences fire on schedule even when you're busy
2. **Tells you where to spend your time** — pipeline stages show you exactly which leads need attention today
3. **Shows you what's working** — source tracking and conversion data let you double down on winners

Here's the 15-minute setup that makes all of this work.

### Step 1: Create These 7 Pipeline Stages (3 minutes)

| Stage | What It Means | Action Required |
|-------|---------------|-----------------|
| **New** | Just imported, not yet contacted | Start Day 1 of cadence |
| **Attempting** | In active 7-day cadence | Continue cadence (automated) |
| **Contacted** | Had a conversation, follow-up needed | Schedule callback or send info |
| **Appointment Set** | Meeting/call scheduled | Prep + confirm 24hrs before |
| **Quoted/Presented** | Gave numbers, waiting on decision | Follow up in 48 hours |
| **Won** | Closed, policy issued, deal funded | Tag source for ROI tracking |
| **Nurture** | Not ready now, might be later | Move to long-tail email drip |

That's it. Seven stages. Every lead lives in one of them at all times. If a lead doesn't fit in any stage, it's dead — remove it.

### Step 2: Set Up 3 Automations (7 minutes)

**Automation 1: New Lead → 7-Day Cadence**
When a lead enters "New," trigger the 7-day follow-up cadence (Day 1 call task + Day 2 email + Day 3 text + etc.). Most CRMs support this natively. In GoHighLevel, it's a workflow trigger.

**Automation 2: No Response After 7 Days → Nurture**
If a lead completes the 7-day cadence without responding, automatically move them to "Nurture" and start a monthly email drip. Don't delete them — 10-15% will convert over the next 90 days from email alone.

**Automation 3: Appointment Set → Confirmation**
When a lead moves to "Appointment Set," automatically send a confirmation text with the date/time and your phone number. Send a reminder 24 hours before.

### Step 3: Import Your Leads With Source Tags (5 minutes)

When you import aged leads, tag them with:
- **Source:** vendor name (e.g., AgedLeadStore)
- **Vertical:** insurance type or loan type
- **Lead age:** 30-day, 60-day, 90-day
- **Import date:** today's date

This seems tedious for 5 minutes of work. It's not. Six months from now, you'll be able to pull a report that shows: "60-day aged final expense leads from AgedLeadStore close at 5.2%, while 90-day aged Medicare leads close at 6.8%." That data determines where every future dollar goes.

### The Compound Effect

This setup takes 15 minutes once. After that:

- Every new lead batch drops into the pipeline and the cadence fires automatically
- You check your CRM each morning, see which leads need callbacks, which appointments are today, and which deals are waiting on decisions
- At the end of the month, you know your cost per deal by source, vertical, and lead age

That's the difference between "working leads" and "running a lead operation."
