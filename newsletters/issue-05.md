## THIS WEEK'S PLAY: The Final Expense Script That Books 1 in 8

Final expense is the highest-volume, lowest-cost corner of the aged lead market. You can buy leads for $0.25-$0.50 each — sometimes less in bulk. At those prices, your math changes completely.

But here's what most agents get wrong: they call a final expense lead the same way they'd call a term life lead. Different buyer. Different conversation. Different script.

Final expense prospects are typically 50-85 years old. They responded to an ad about covering funeral costs so their family wouldn't have to. That's not a financial planning conversation — that's an emotional one.

**The script that books 1 appointment per 8 conversations:**

> "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name]. You filled out a form a little while back about making sure your family wouldn't have to pay for funeral expenses out of pocket. I'm just following up on that — is that still something you've been thinking about?"

That's the opener. Here's why every word matters:

- **"A little while back"** — acknowledges the lead is aged without drawing attention to it
- **"Making sure your family wouldn't have to pay"** — mirrors their original motivation, not yours
- **"Is that still something you've been thinking about?"** — yes/no question that gets them talking

If they say yes (and 50-60% of conversations will), the next line is:

> "Good — most people I talk to are surprised at how affordable it actually is. If I could show you a plan that covers everything for [estimate $30-50/month based on age], would it be worth 10 minutes of your time this week?"

**The Numbers That Make This Work**

| Metric | Final Expense Aged Leads |
|--------|------------------------|
| Cost per lead | $0.25-$0.50 |
| Contact rate (7-day cadence) | 30-40% |
| Conversation-to-appointment | 40-50% |
| Appointment-to-close | 55-65% |

At $0.35/lead and a 4-5% close rate, you're spending **$7-$9 per closed policy** that pays $600-$900 in commission. That's a 70-100x return.

The key is volume. You're not closing 1 in 4 like fresh exclusive leads. You're closing 1 in 20. But at $0.35 each, buying 100 leads costs $35. Fresh final expense leads cost $15-$25 each — you'd spend $1,500-$2,500 for the same 100.

**What Kills Final Expense Campaigns**

Three mistakes I see repeatedly:

1. **Speaking too fast.** Your prospect is likely older. Slow down. Use shorter sentences. Pause after questions.
2. **Leading with price before empathy.** The monthly premium matters, but the emotional trigger — "so your kids don't have to worry about this" — is what gets the appointment.
3. **Giving up after Day 3.** Final expense prospects are often home during the day but screen calls. The Day 5-7 texts and the breakup message on Day 7 consistently produce the highest callback rates in this vertical.

### THE SCRIPT

**Full final expense phone script — aged leads:**

**[Opening]**
"Hi, is this [Name]? Great — this is [Your Name] with [Company]. You requested some information a while back about covering final expenses so your family wouldn't be out of pocket. Is that still something on your mind?"

**[If yes]**
"I hear that a lot. Most folks I talk to just want to make sure their family isn't stuck with a $10,000-$15,000 bill. The good news is there are plans that cover everything — funeral, burial, any outstanding bills — for somewhere around $30-$50 a month depending on your age and health. No medical exam required for most plans."

**[Book the appointment]**
"What I'd like to do is take about 10 minutes and show you exactly what's available at your age. I can do that over the phone — would [tomorrow/day after] morning or afternoon work better for you?"

**[If not sure / hesitant]**
"Totally understand. There's no obligation at all. Most people just want to see what the numbers look like so they can make an informed decision. Would it help if I sent you some information by email first, and then we could chat after you've had a chance to look it over?"
