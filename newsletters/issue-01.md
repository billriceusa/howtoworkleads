## THIS WEEK'S PLAY: The Real Math on Aged vs. Fresh Leads

Most agents think aged leads are where careers go to die. They picture dusty spreadsheets of disconnected numbers.

Here's the truth: aged leads cost 80-95% less than fresh leads — and the people who requested them still need insurance, still need a mortgage, still need solar. They didn't stop needing those things. They just stopped hearing from agents.

**The math that should change how you spend your lead budget:**

- A fresh exclusive lead costs $15-50 depending on vertical
- An aged lead (30-90 days old) costs $0.15-2.00
- That means you can buy 10-30x more aged leads for the same budget

"But the contact rate is lower!" — yes. And it still works in your favor.

**Here's a real comparison:**

| Metric | Fresh Leads (100) | Aged Leads (1,000) |
|--------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Cost | $2,500 | $500 |
| Contact rate | 45% | 12% |
| Contacts made | 45 | 120 |
| Close rate | 10% | 4% |
| Deals closed | 4.5 | 4.8 |
| Cost per deal | $556 | $104 |

Same number of deals. One-fifth the cost. That's not a compromise — that's a strategy.

The agents who crush it with aged leads aren't better closers. They have a better *system*: a 7-day multi-channel cadence, a CRM that doesn't let leads slip through, and scripts built for people who already expressed interest once.

**The #1 mistake?** Treating aged leads like cold calls. These people raised their hand. Your job isn't to convince them they have a need — it's to show up at the right time with the right offer.

## THE DATA

**Aged lead conversion rates by vertical (industry averages):**

- Life insurance: 3-5%
- Final expense: 4-7%
- Medicare: 5-8%
- Auto insurance: 5-8%
- Mortgage (purchase): 2-4%
- Mortgage (refinance): 1-3%
- Solar: 2-4%

These numbers come from agents working 30-90 day old leads with a structured follow-up cadence. Without a cadence, conversion drops to under 1%.
