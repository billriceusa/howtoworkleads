## THIS WEEK'S PLAY: Real Numbers From 100 Aged Insurance Leads

Everybody talks about conversion rates. Almost nobody shares actual data from a real campaign.

So let's fix that. Here are the numbers from a 100-lead test batch of aged insurance leads — 60-90 days old, mixed verticals (life, final expense, Medicare), purchased from a reputable vendor at $0.75/lead.

**Total investment: $75.**

### The Funnel

| Stage | Count | Rate |
|-------|-------|------|
| Leads purchased | 100 | — |
| Valid phone numbers (post-DNC scrub) | 87 | 87% |
| Reached by phone, text, or email | 31 | 36% of valid |
| Had a meaningful conversation (2+ min) | 18 | 58% of reached |
| Booked an appointment | 9 | 50% of conversations |
| Showed up to appointment | 7 | 78% of booked |
| Submitted application | 5 | 71% of showed |
| Issued/bound policy | 4 | 80% of submitted |

**4 policies from $75 in leads.** Average commission per policy: $1,200.

**Total commission: $4,800. ROI: 6,300%.**

Now — is that typical? It's on the higher end. A more conservative run might yield 2-3 policies from 100 leads. Even at 2 policies ($2,400), the ROI is still 3,200%.

### What Made This Work

Three things separated this from a "dial and pray" campaign:

**1. DNC scrubbing before the first call.** 13 of the 100 leads were on the federal DNC registry. Calling them would have been a $500-$43,000 per call liability. Five minutes of scrubbing eliminated that risk entirely.

**2. The 7-day multi-channel cadence.** Of the 31 contacts made, 11 came on Day 1. The other 20 came on Days 2-7. Without the cadence, this campaign closes 1-2 policies instead of 4.

**3. Vertical-specific scripts.** The Medicare scripts referenced enrollment periods. The final expense scripts led with family protection. The life insurance scripts focused on rate changes. Generic scripts don't work on aged leads — specificity does.

### The Leads That Didn't Convert

What about the 96 that didn't become policies?

- 13 removed by DNC scrub (never called)
- 56 never reached (disconnected, no answer after 7 attempts, wrong number)
- 13 reached but not interested ("already handled it," "don't remember requesting info")
- 10 interested but not ready (moved to long-tail nurture email drip)
- 4 converted to policies

Those 10 nurture leads are still in the pipeline. Historically, 1-2 of them will convert over the next 90 days from the email drip alone. That brings the likely final count to 5-6 policies from the original 100 leads.

## THE STACK

- **Stat:** The average cost of a TCPA violation is $500-$1,500 per call. On a 100-lead campaign, skipping DNC scrubbing could expose you to $6,500-$19,500 in liability from those 13 leads alone. Scrubbing takes 5 minutes.
- **Tool:** [DNC.com](https://www.dnc.com) for federal DNC scrubbing. Most vendors also offer state-level DNC scrubbing. Budget $0.01-0.03 per number.
- **Compliance:** The [FCC 1:1 consent rule](https://www.howtoworkleads.com/blog/fcc-one-to-one-consent-rule?utm_source=newsletter&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=aged-lead-playbook&utm_content=issue-03-stack-fcc) means every lead must have consented to hear from *you specifically*. Aged leads from reputable vendors handle this — but ask your vendor for documentation.
