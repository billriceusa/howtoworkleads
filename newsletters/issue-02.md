## THIS WEEK'S PLAY: The 7-Day Multi-Channel Cadence

Here's the brutal math on aged lead follow-up: **80% of sales require 5+ contacts, but 44% of agents stop after one.**

That gap is where the money lives.

I'm going to walk you through the exact 7-day cadence that consistently produces 2-3x the contact rate of a "call once, leave a voicemail, move on" approach. This cadence uses three channels — phone, text, and email — timed to maximize your chances of catching someone at the right moment.

**The rules before we start:**
- Never skip a day. Consistency beats intensity.
- Personalize every touchpoint with their name and original inquiry.
- Vary the time of day across the week.
- If they respond at any point — stop the cadence and shift to conversation.

**Day 1 (Morning — 10 AM local time): Call + Voicemail + Text**

Call first. If no answer, leave a 20-second voicemail — friendly, specific to their inquiry, and end with "I'll shoot you a quick text with my info." Then send the text immediately.

This triple-touch on Day 1 sets the tone. You're professional, you're accessible, and you're not going away.

**Day 2 (Afternoon — 2 PM): Email**

Subject line: "[First Name], still looking for [product type]?"

The email should be 3-4 sentences. Acknowledge their original inquiry. Offer one specific value point. Include your phone number and a "reply to this email" CTA.

**Day 3 (Morning — 9 AM): Text only**

Keep it short: "Hey [Name], this is [You] — I left you a voicemail yesterday about your [insurance/mortgage] inquiry. Still looking? Happy to help if so."

Texts have a 98% open rate. By Day 3, they've seen your name three times. You're becoming familiar.

**Day 4 (Afternoon — 4 PM): Call + Voicemail**

Different time of day than Day 1. Many people who don't answer mornings will pick up late afternoon. New voicemail — don't repeat the Day 1 script. Reference something specific: a rate, a plan option, a savings estimate.

**Day 5 (Morning — 10 AM): Email**

This one provides value. Share a relevant data point or tip. "Agents are seeing 4-7% close rates on aged Medicare leads right now. If you're still exploring options, I can run some quick numbers for you."

**Day 6 (Rest day):** No contact. Let the previous touches breathe.

**Day 7 (Morning — 9 AM): Final call + breakup text**

Last attempt. Call, and if no answer, send the "breakup" text: "Hey [Name], I've reached out a few times about your [product] inquiry. I don't want to be a pest — if you've already got this handled, no worries at all. But if something changes down the road, you have my number. All the best."

This text has the highest callback rate of the entire cadence. People respond to the door closing.

## THE TEMPLATE

**Copy-paste breakup text (Day 7):**

> Hey [Name], I've reached out a few times about your [insurance type / mortgage / solar] inquiry from a while back. If you're all set, totally understand — I'll stop bugging you. But if something changes, I'm here. Either way, wishing you the best. — [Your Name], [Your Number]

**Why it works:** It's human. It's respectful. And it triggers loss aversion — the option is about to disappear.

Agents who use this text report a 15-20% callback rate on leads that were otherwise unresponsive.
