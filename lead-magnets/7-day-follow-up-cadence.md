# The 7-Day Aged Lead Follow-Up Cadence

**A Day-by-Day Multi-Channel Plan to Convert Aged Leads Into Appointments**

*From HowToWorkLeads.com — The Aged Lead Playbook*

---

## Why This Cadence Works

Most agents make one call, leave one voicemail, and move on. That's a 1-touch approach in a world that requires 5-12 touches. This cadence uses three channels — phone, text, and email — across seven days to maximize your contact rate and position you as a professional, not a spammer.

**Rules of the cadence:**
- Never skip a day (consistency beats intensity)
- Always personalize with the prospect's name and their original inquiry
- Vary the time of day across the week
- If they respond at any point, shift to a conversational flow — stop the cadence

## INSURANCE VERSION

### Day 1 — Call + Voicemail + Text (10:00 AM local)

**Call Script:**
> "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company]. I'm calling because you looked into [life insurance / health coverage / Medicare] a little while back. I wanted to check — did you ever find the right plan, or is that still on your list?"

**Voicemail (if no answer):**
> "Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company]. I'm reaching out because you inquired about [insurance type] a while ago. I'm not sure if you got that handled, but I had a couple of options that might save you some money. I'll send you a quick text with my info — feel free to call or text me back at [number]. Talk soon."

**Text (immediately after voicemail):**
> "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company]. I just left you a voicemail about the [insurance type] you looked into. No pressure — just wanted to see if you still need help finding the right plan. Text me back anytime."

---

### Day 2 — Email (8:00 AM local)

**Subject line:** "Quick question about your [insurance type] search"

**Body:**
> Hi [Name],
>
> I tried reaching you yesterday — I know you looked into [life insurance / health coverage / Medicare options] a while back, and I wanted to see if you ever found what you were looking for.
>
> If you're still exploring, I'd love to run a quick comparison for you. Most people I work with find a plan that saves them $30-50/month in about 10 minutes.
>
> No obligation — just reply to this email or call/text me at [number].
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]
> [Company]
> [Phone]

---

### Day 3 — Call Only (4:30 PM local)

**Script (different angle):**
> "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] — I reached out a couple of days ago about [insurance type]. I know you're busy so I'll be quick: I've been helping people in [their area/state] find better rates this month, and I wanted to make sure you didn't miss out. Do you have 2 minutes?"

**If voicemail:** Hang up. No message today — you'll text tomorrow. Leaving a voicemail every day feels pushy; strategic silence creates curiosity.

---

### Day 4 — Text Message (11:00 AM local)

> "Hey [Name], just checking in. I've been able to save most folks in [state/area] around $30-50/mo on their [insurance type] coverage. Worth a quick look? I can send you a no-obligation quote. Just reply YES and I'll get it started."

---

### Day 5 — Email (9:00 AM local)

**Subject line:** "Saved [Client first name] $47/month — thought of you"

**Body:**
> Hi [Name],
>
> I just helped a client in [state/city] save $47/month on their [insurance type] coverage by switching to a plan that actually fit their situation better. It took us about 15 minutes.
>
> I don't know if you're still looking, but if you are, I'd be happy to do the same comparison for you — no cost, no pressure.
>
> Reply here or text me at [number].
>
> [Your Name]

---

### Day 6 — Call + Voicemail (12:00 PM local)

**Voicemail:**
> "Hi [Name], it's [Your Name] from [Company] — this is my last call on this. I've been trying to reach you about the [insurance type] you looked into, and I don't want to be a pest. If you found coverage already, great — I'm happy for you. If not, my number is [number] and I can still get you a free comparison. Either way, I wish you the best. Take care."

**Note:** The "last call" positioning is powerful. It removes pressure and often triggers a callback because people feel the window closing.

---

### Day 7 — Break-Up Email (8:00 AM local)

**Subject line:** "Closing your file"

**Body:**
> Hi [Name],
>
> I've reached out a few times about the [insurance type] coverage you were looking into, and I haven't heard back — which is totally fine. Everyone's on their own timeline.
>
> I'm going to close out your file on my end, but if anything changes down the road — rates go up, your situation changes, or you just want a second opinion — I'm here. Just reply to this email.
>
> Wishing you all the best,
> [Your Name]
> [Company]
> [Phone]

**Why "closing your file" works:** It's the single highest-response email in most follow-up sequences. The fear of missing out on a resource (you) triggers replies from prospects who were on the fence.

## After the 7-Day Cadence

**Don't delete these leads.** Move them into a long-term nurture:

| Week | Action |
|---|---|
| Week 2 | One email with valuable content (market update, tips) |
| Week 3 | One text check-in |
| Week 4 | One email with a case study or testimonial |
| Month 2-3 | Monthly email newsletter |
| Month 3+ | Quarterly personal check-in call |

**Key stat:** 20-30% of aged lead conversions happen after the initial 7-day cadence, during the nurture phase. The agents who maintain a long-term drip on their aged lead database consistently outperform those who only work the first week.

## Timing Cheat Sheet

| Time of Day | Best For | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 8:00 - 9:00 AM | Emails | Caught during morning inbox check |
| 10:00 - 11:00 AM | Calls | People are awake, settled into their day |
| 11:00 AM - 12:00 PM | Texts | Mid-morning, phone is nearby |
| 4:00 - 5:30 PM | Calls | End of workday, more relaxed |

**Avoid:** Before 8 AM, during lunch (12-1 PM), and after 8 PM. Weekends are fine for texts and emails but skip phone calls unless you're in a market where Saturday works well (real estate, solar).

## Tracking Your Results

For every batch of aged leads, track these metrics:

- **Contact rate:** % of leads you actually spoke to (target: 10-15%)
- **Appointment rate:** % of contacts that booked a call/meeting (target: 20-30% of contacts)
- **Close rate:** % of appointments that became clients (target: 15-25%)
- **Cost per acquisition:** Lead cost / number of closed deals
- **Best-performing day/channel:** Which touchpoint generated the most responses?

Review weekly. Adjust scripts, timing, and channel mix based on what your data tells you.
