## How to Use This Bundle

These scripts are starting points, not straitjackets. Customize them with your name, company, specific insurance products, and local details. The structure and psychology behind each script is what matters — the exact words should sound like you.

**Golden rules for aged insurance leads:**
- Acknowledge the time gap ("I know this was a while ago")
- Lead with their problem, not your product
- Offer value before asking for commitment
- Keep it conversational, not corporate

## PHONE SCRIPTS

### 1. Cold Call Opener (Standard Aged Lead)

> "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company]. I'm reaching out because you requested information about [life insurance / health insurance / Medicare / final expense] a while back. I'm not sure if you ever found the right plan — did you get that taken care of, or is it still something you're working on?"

**Why it works:** The question "did you get that taken care of?" gives them an easy out, which paradoxically makes them more likely to engage honestly.

### 2. Warm Lead Opener (Showed Prior Interest or Partial Application)

> "Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company]. I see you started looking into [insurance type] a few months ago — it looks like you might have been comparing options or started an application but didn't finish. That happens all the time, especially when there are so many choices. I was wondering if you'd like me to simplify it and walk you through your top 2-3 options. It takes about 10 minutes."

### 3. Follow-Up Call (After Previous Contact Attempt)

> "Hi [Name], it's [Your Name] calling back. I reached out [yesterday / a couple days ago] about the [insurance type] you were looking into. I had a chance to pull together a couple of options for someone in your area and age range — I think you'd be surprised at the pricing. Do you have 5 minutes for me to walk you through it?"

### 4. Re-Engagement Call (60+ Day Old Lead)

> "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company]. I know it's been a while since you first looked into [insurance type], and a lot has changed since then — new plans, new pricing, some really good options that weren't available before. I'm doing a round of courtesy calls to people who were interested but might not have found the right fit yet. Is now a good time for a quick chat?"

## VOICEMAIL SCRIPTS

### 5. Voicemail — First Attempt (Friendly + Direct)

> "Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company]. You looked into [insurance type] a while back, and I wanted to see if you still need help with that. I've got a couple of options that might save you some money. My number is [number] — I'll also send you a text so you have it. Hope to hear from you."

### 6. Voicemail — Second Attempt (Add Value)

> "Hi [Name], [Your Name] again from [Company]. I ran some numbers for people in [state/area] your age range and found plans starting at [price range]. I don't know your full situation yet, but I'd love to see if we can beat whatever you're paying now — or get you covered if you haven't found a plan yet. Call or text me at [number]."

### 7. Voicemail — Final Attempt (Soft Close)

> "Hi [Name], it's [Your Name] from [Company]. This is my last message — I don't want to keep bugging you. If you already found coverage, that's great. If not, I'm here whenever you're ready. My number is [number], and I mean it — even if it's six months from now, call me. I'll find you the right plan. Take care."

## OBJECTION HANDLING

### 8. "I already have insurance."

> "That's great — I'm glad you're covered. Honest question though: when's the last time you compared what you're paying to what's available now? A lot of my clients come to me thinking they have a good deal, and we end up finding them the same or better coverage for $30-50 less per month. Would it be worth a free comparison just to see?"

### 9. "I'm not interested."

> "Totally fair. Can I ask you one quick question before I let you go? Was it that you found a plan already, or that the timing just wasn't right? [Pause and let them answer.] The reason I ask is that most people who looked into this still have the same need — it just got pushed to the back burner. If I could show you options in under 5 minutes, would that be worth your time?"

### 10. "How did you get my number?"

> "Great question. You filled out an online form a while back requesting information about [insurance type]. Your info came to us because we specialize in helping people find the best rates. I completely understand if you don't remember — it was a while ago. I'm just following up to see if you still need help with that. If not, I'll remove you from our list — no problem at all."

**Key:** Stay calm and transparent. Never be defensive. Offer to remove them — this often de-escalates the moment and they end up continuing the conversation.

### 11. "Just send me information."

> "Absolutely, I can do that. What's the best email address? [Get email.] While I have you — so I send you the right info — can I ask two quick questions? What type of coverage are you looking for, and is there a budget range you're trying to stay within? That way I don't waste your time with plans that aren't relevant."

**Why this works:** You agree to their request (builds trust), get their email (another channel), and then naturally transition into a qualifying conversation.

### 12. "I need to think about it."

> "Of course — this is an important decision. What specifically do you want to think over? Is it the price, the coverage, or something else? [Let them answer.] I ask because a lot of times I can clear up a concern right now and save you the back-and-forth. And if you still need time after that, no pressure at all — I'll follow up in a few days."

## TEXT MESSAGE TEMPLATES

### 13. First Contact Text

> "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company]. You looked into [insurance type] a while back — I wanted to see if you still need help finding the right plan. I can run a free quote comparison for you. Text me back if you're interested!"

### 14. Follow-Up Text (After No Response)

> "Hey [Name], just following up. I specialize in helping people find affordable [insurance type] coverage. Most of my clients save $30-50/month when they compare. Worth a quick look? Reply YES and I'll get started."

### 15. Value-Add Text

> "Hi [Name], quick heads up — [open enrollment is coming up / rates are changing next month / there's a new plan available in your state]. If you're still looking for [insurance type], now's a great time to lock in. Want me to run your numbers?"

### 16. Social Proof Text

> "[Name], just helped a [man/woman] in [state] save $52/month on their [insurance type]. Took about 10 minutes. Want me to see what I can do for you? No obligation."

### 17. Break-Up Text

> "Hi [Name], I've reached out a few times about [insurance type] — I'll stop here so I'm not bugging you. If you ever want a free quote comparison, save my number. Best of luck! — [Your Name]"

>

>

> 2. **Not comparing across carriers.** Rates for the same coverage can vary by 40% or more between companies.
> 3. **Waiting too long.** Especially with [life / health] insurance, every year you wait can mean higher premiums. Locking in a rate now saves you money for the life of the policy.
>
> If you want, I can run a side-by-side comparison for you — takes about 10 minutes and there's no cost or obligation.
>
> Just reply to this email or call me at [number].
>
> [Your Name]

## Script Customization Tips

**By product line:**
- **Life insurance:** Emphasize protection for family, locking in rates while healthy, affordability ("costs less than your streaming subscriptions")
- **Health insurance:** Focus on coverage gaps, network access, out-of-pocket maximums, prescription coverage
- **Medicare:** Lead with confusion reduction ("I help simplify your Medicare choices"), emphasize plan comparison and cost savings
- **Final expense:** Be respectful and gentle, focus on relieving the burden on family, emphasize no medical exam options

**By lead age:**
- **30-60 days:** Reference their inquiry directly — "You looked into this recently"
- **60-90 days:** Softer approach — "A while back you were exploring options"
- **90-180 days:** Lead with what's changed — "A lot has changed since you first looked into this"

**By time of day:**
- **Morning calls (9-11 AM):** More professional tone, respect their workday
- **Afternoon calls (4-6 PM):** More relaxed, conversational tone
- **Texts anytime:** Keep them under 160 characters when possible for deliverability
