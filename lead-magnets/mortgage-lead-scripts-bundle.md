## Before You Dial: Mortgage-Specific Principles

Aged mortgage leads are different from aged insurance leads. The prospect's situation — rates, home values, their credit, their employment — may have genuinely changed since they inquired. Use that to your advantage.

**Mortgage script principles:**
- Reference rate movement (up or down — both create urgency)
- Never quote a specific rate on the first call — offer to "run their scenario"
- Ask about their timeline, not just their interest
- Pre-qualify conversationally, not like a loan application
- Always include your NMLS number in written communications

## PHONE SCRIPTS

### 1. Purchase Lead Opener

> "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company], NMLS [number]. You were looking into a home purchase a while back, and I wanted to check in. Are you still house hunting, or did you already find a place?"

**If still looking:** "Great — what's been the biggest challenge? Finding the right home, or getting comfortable with the financing?"

**If found a home:** "Congrats! Did you already lock in your financing, or are you still shopping lenders? I ask because I've been able to beat initial quotes for a lot of my clients — even at this stage."

**If stopped looking:** "Totally understand. Market's been tricky. Out of curiosity, what made you pause — was it pricing, rates, or just life getting in the way? [Let them answer.] Things have actually shifted since then. Would it be worth 5 minutes to see where you'd stand today?"

### 2. Refinance Lead Opener

> "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company]. You looked into refinancing your mortgage a while ago, and I know rates have been all over the place since then. I wanted to see where things stand for you — are you still thinking about it?"

**Rate-environment language that works regardless of market:**

- **Rates have dropped:** "Rates have come down since you first looked, and I think the numbers might work now even if they didn't before."
- **Rates have risen:** "I know rates aren't where anyone wants them, but depending on your current rate and situation, there may still be a smart play — cash-out, shorter term, or dropping PMI."
- **Rates are flat:** "Rates have stabilized, which actually gives us a cleaner picture of what makes sense. Sometimes the best refi decisions happen in a stable market."

### 3. DSCR / Investor Loan Opener

> "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company]. I see you were looking into financing for an investment property. I specialize in investor loans — DSCR, bank statement, asset-based programs — and I wanted to check in. Are you still looking at properties, or did you already close on something?"

**If active:** "What type of property are you targeting — single-family, multi-unit, short-term rental? And do you have a property under contract, or are you still looking? I ask because I can get you a pre-approval letter that works with your investment strategy."

### 4. Follow-Up Call (After Previous Attempt)

> "Hi [Name], it's [Your Name] calling back from [Company]. I reached out [yesterday / earlier this week] about your mortgage inquiry. I pulled some rough numbers based on what you were looking for, and I think there's a good option here. Do you have a couple of minutes? I promise I'm not going to ask for your social security number on this call — just a conversation."

**The light humor about SSN works because it addresses the unspoken fear** that the call will turn into a loan application. It sets them at ease.

## VOICEMAIL SCRIPTS

### 5. Voicemail — First Attempt

> "Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company], NMLS [number]. I'm calling because you inquired about a [home purchase / refinance] a while back, and I wanted to see if the timing might be better now. I'd love to see if the same applies to you. No application needed, just a quick conversation. [Number]. Talk soon."

No expiration on that. Take care."

## OBJECTION HANDLING

### 8. "I'm already working with a lender."

> "That's great — having a lender is the right first step. Can I ask, have you locked your rate yet? [If no:] Okay, so you're still in a position to compare. Here's what I'd suggest — let me run your scenario independently. If I can beat what they've offered, you have leverage either way. If I can't, you'll know you're getting a good deal. Either way you win. Fair?"

> [If yes, already locked:] "Smart move. I'll make a note — when's your lock expiration? If anything changes or you need a backup option, I'm a phone call away. And for your next purchase or refi, keep my number handy."

### 9. "Rates are too high right now."

> "I hear that a lot, and honestly, compared to two years ago, you're right — they're higher. But here's the thing: [For purchase:] home prices are actually [leveling off / offering more negotiation room] because of those rates. Buyers who move now are competing against fewer people. The play is: buy at today's price, refinance when rates come down. You marry the house, you date the rate. [For refi:] it depends on what you're trying to accomplish. If it's a cash-out to consolidate debt at 22% interest into a mortgage at 7%, the math still works in your favor. Want me to run your specific scenario?"

### 10. "I don't think I'll qualify."

> "I appreciate you being upfront about that. Here's what I've found — about half the people who tell me they won't qualify actually do, because there are more programs than most people realize. FHA, VA, bank statement loans, asset-based programs, even credit repair pathways that take 60-90 days. Can I ask a couple of questions to see where you stand? No credit pull needed for a preliminary look."

### 11. "I need to talk to my spouse / partner."

> "Absolutely — this is a joint decision, and I'd actually love to include them. What if we set up a quick 15-minute call with both of you? I can walk you through the numbers together so you're on the same page. What day works best?"

### 12. "Just send me the rates."

> "I wish I could give you a one-size-fits-all rate sheet, but mortgage rates are really specific to your situation — credit, down payment, property type, loan amount. What I can do is run your scenario in about 5 minutes and send you a custom rate quote that's actually accurate. Can I ask you a few quick questions to get that started?"

## PRE-QUALIFICATION QUESTIONS SCRIPT

### 13. Conversational Pre-Qual (Use After Building Rapport)

> "Great — let me ask you a few quick questions so I can give you accurate numbers. This isn't an application — I'm just trying to get us in the right ballpark."

1. "Are you looking to purchase or refinance?"
2. **Purchase:** "Do you have a property in mind, or are you still looking? What price range?" / **Refi:** "What's your current rate, and roughly what do you owe?"
3. "What area are you looking in?" (state + county matters for rates)
4. "How much are you looking to put down?" / "Do you have equity you'd like to tap into?"
5. "Is this your primary residence, second home, or investment property?"
6. "Employment — are you W-2 salaried, self-employed, or a mix?"
7. "Ballpark — where's your credit score? Above 740, 700-740, or below 700?"
8. "Is there a timeline you're working with?"

> "Perfect — that gives me everything I need to pull together real numbers for you. Can I call you back [tomorrow / later today] with a couple of options?"

**Note:** Never ask for SSN, DOB, or run credit during the first conversation. The goal is to build trust and qualify — the application comes after they choose to work with you.

## Rate Language That Works in Any Market

The biggest mistake mortgage LOs make with aged leads is apologizing for rates. Instead, use language that reframes rates as context, not a barrier.

| Situation | What to Say |
|---|---|
| Rates are high | "Rates are higher than they were, but competition is lower — that means more negotiating power on price. Buy now, refi later." |
| Rates are dropping | "Rates have come down since you first looked — this might be the window you were waiting for." |
| Rates are flat | "The market has stabilized, which is actually a great time to lock in — no more guessing games." |
| Client has a high current rate | "If you're sitting at [X]% or above, even a small reduction could save you hundreds per month." |
| Client has a low current rate | "Your rate is solid — but if you need cash for [renovation / debt consolidation / investment], a cash-out refi at today's rates could still make sense depending on the math." |

**Universal closer for any rate conversation:**
> "Here's what I always tell people: I can't control rates, but I can control finding you the absolute best rate available for your situation, today. Let me prove it."
